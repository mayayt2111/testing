Traverse Kamakura Kokomae Station, Kotoku-in & Enoshima

Kamakura Kokomae Station

We visited the Kamakura Kokomae Station, which is home to the Slam Dunk scene. The weather was pleasant and cool, allowing us to take beautiful photos without excessive sweating. And here are the pictures we took with kimono(Japanese Traditional clothes):D

Kotoku-in

After the station, we went to the Kotoku-in, a temple with a giant bronze Buddha statue. Since it's now June, the hydrangeas are in full bloom, making it an ideal time for a temple visit and flower viewing.

Enoshima

Our final stop was Enoshima, an island just a 15-minute walk from Enoshima Station. This is a great time of year to visit, as the Flore Garden at the top of the hill near the sea is in bloom from May to July. Enoshima is known for its fresh seafood, as it is situated next to the Pacific Ocean. We were able to enjoy the freshest sashimi and other marine delicacies.
